🐍 Snakes and Stairs — Full Official Game Rules 🪜

1. Game Overview

"Snakes and Stairs" is a turn-based, competitive board game designed for 2 to 4 players. Inspired by the classic Snakes and Ladders concept, this version introduces unique tactical abilities for human players to alter the course of the game. The goal is to navigate your character token from tile 0 to the exact target tile dictated by the chosen difficulty level.
The game supports local multiplayer where you can play against other humans or automated computer opponents.
Important Note: COMPUTER bots are not AI. They are basic, automated player profiles governed by simple random number generation scripts and do not strategically use any tactical abilities.


2. Main Menu and System Modes

When you launch the game, you can navigate through the following system modes:
* Game: Launches the configuration setup to start a live match.
• Rules: The mode provides players with a quick guide to the game's mechanics, winning conditions, and menu navigation.
* Highscores: Displays a persistent scoreboard containing all registered players and their cumulative points saved across matches, sorted from highest to lowest.
* Settings: Allows players to swap the active language interface between English and Russian (Русский) at any time.
* Exit: Safely closes the game application after a confirmation prompt.


3. Match Setup and Turn Order

* Player Selection: You can choose to play with 2, 3, or 4 players. "Player 1" is always a Human player who must input a nickname. For the remaining slots, you can input names for other human players. Leaving a name prompt completely blank automatically fills that slot with an automated bot (e.g., `COMPUTER1`, `COMPUTER2`, etc.). New player names are automatically registered into the leaderboard system.
* The Moment of Truth (Turn Order): Before movement begins, all players roll a single 6-sided dice simultaneously. The game ranks players in descending order based on their rolls to determine the final turn sequence. If any players roll matching numbers, the tie-breaker triggers an automatic re-roll for everyone until all initial rolls are completely unique.


4. Game Parameters (Difficulties and Board Sizes)

At the start of a match, players select a game parameter which determines the layout of obstacles and the exact winning tile:
* Easy Mode (Target: Tile 50)
 		Standard Snakes: Tiles 13 and 31
 		Long Snakes: Tile 47
 		Standard Stairs: Tiles 8 and 38
 		Long Stairs: Tile 22

* Normal Mode (Target: Tile 75)
 		Standard Snakes: Tiles 25, 36, and 49
 		Long Snakes: Tiles 73 and 68
 		Standard Stairs: Tiles 20, 38, and 57
 		Long Stairs: Tiles 3 and 12

* Hard Mode (Target: Tile 100)
 		Standard Snakes: Tiles 24, 64, 63, and 62
 		Long Snakes: Tiles 13, 49, and 80
 		Dangerous Snake: Tile 95
 		Standard Stairs: Tiles 4, 32, 70, and 61
 		Long Stairs: Tiles 15, 55, and 87


5. Core Movement and Board Obstacles

On your turn, a standard 6-sided dice is rolled (generating a value from 1 to 6) to advance your token. Automated bots roll immediately, while human players have the option to input special commands before rolling. Landing on a tile with an obstacle applies an instantaneous penalty or boost:
* Standard Stairs (🪜): Moves your token forward by 6 additional tiles.
* Long Stairs (🪜🪜): Moves your token forward by 12 additional tiles.
* Standard Snakes (🐍): Slips your token backward by 6 tiles.
* Long Snakes (🐍🐍): Slips your token backward by 12 tiles.
* Dangerous Snake (Dangerous 🐍): Exclusive to Hard Mode, landing here inflicts a massive penalty, sending your token backward by 60 tiles.
* Exact Landing Rule: To claim a winning position, a player's final movement must land precisely on the target tile (50, 75, or 100). If a dice roll causes you to overshoot the target tile, the movement is completely invalidated, the game notifies you that the number is bigger than the parameter, and your token remains stationary on its original tile for that turn.


6. Special Tactical Abilities (Human Players Only)

Every Human player begins a match equipped with a kit of 4 unique, single-use abilities. Automated Computer bots cannot use abilities. Activating an ability consumes it for the remainder of the match. Human players can activate the following keywords on their turn:
* 🌀 teleport: Instantly swaps your current tile position with the tile position of any chosen opponent. Using a teleportation consumes your turn and skips your standard dice roll phase.
* ✖️ double: Doubling your roll ensures that whatever number you get on your subsequent dice roll for that turn is multiplied by 2 (e.g., rolling a 4 advances you 8 tiles).
* 🚀 rocket: Instantly propels your token forward by 10 tiles. This ability can only be triggered if the 10-tile boost keeps you safely below the target winning tile; it will refuse to activate if it causes you to reach or overshoot the final parameter. After the rocket activates, you still proceed to roll your standard dice for that turn.
* 🧊 ice: Freezes an opponent of your choice. On that targeted opponent's immediate next turn, they are marked as "iced", meaning they are skipped entirely, unable to move or use abilities. They automatically thaw out after their skipped turn concludes.


7. Winning, Placements, and Scoring

The match runs continuously until all top placements are decided and recorded:
* Winning Conditions: Placements are locked in sequence as players cross the exact target tile line. The game distributes titles in order: First Winner, Second Winner, and Third Winner.
* Base Points Allocation: 
		1st Place: Earns 3 points.
		2nd Place: Earns 2 points.
		3rd Place: Earns 1 point.
		4th Place: Earns 0 points.

* No-Ability Multiplier Bonus: If a Human player achieves 1st place and wins the match without having spent any of their 4 starting tactical abilities, they receive a double points bonus—rewarding them with 6 leaderboard points instead of 3.
* Match Termination: A 2-player match ends as soon as 1st place is decided. A 3-player match ends when 1st and 2nd places are secured. A 4-player match ends once 1st, 2nd, and 3rd places are filled. All points earned are instantly saved to the global database profile for bragging rights on the Highscores leaderboard.
